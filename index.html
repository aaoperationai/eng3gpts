PART ONE The rules that never bend
01

Non-Negotiable Rules

Two rules sit above everything else in this deck. Break either one and nothing the tool gives you can be trusted.

NON-NEGOTIABLE The golden rule
DRAFT NOT FOR PUBLICATION
01
Rule 01

Every output
is a draft.

Everything the three GPTs produce is a preliminary draft. Not a single line goes live on any AA platform without human review.

Where it bites hardest: the URGENT and wire desks — speed tempts you to publish straight away. The machine writes; the human decides.
NON-NEGOTIABLE The red line
02
Rule 02

Source fidelity.

The GPT writes only from the material you give it — no number, name, date, or quote from outside the text.

The dangerous failure: the model adds a plausible-sounding figure or background that simply isn't in the source. Check every number and every name against the source before anything else.
PART TWO One job, one tool
02

Choosing the Right Tool

Three tools, three different jobs. Open the wrong one and you fight the model instead of using it.

THE CORE DIFFERENCE Editor · URGENT · BREAKING

Same desk. Three jobs.

DISCIPLINE Rule 04
04
BREAKING desk

Never promote an
urgent into a breaking.

If the material is only reactions and detail, the correct BREAKING output is No breaking line.

That is not a failure — it's the model behaving correctly. Reaction and detail belong in URGENT, not BREAKING.
PART THREE Day-to-day handling
03

Running Them Properly

Three habits that decide whether the tool helps you or wastes your time.

THREE HABITS Rules 05 – 07

Get the input right.

05

The three-question gate

On the first material, Editor asks: story, X post, or edit? Don't skip it — or name the task up front ("write the story") to save time.

06

Input quality = output quality

Incomplete material means incomplete output. When Editor writes "Missing data:", complete the source — don't ignore it.

07

English, any input

Paste Arabic or Turkish — the result comes out in English. Translation is smooth, not word-for-word. Always review the translated quotes.

PART FOUR Your job, not the model's
04

Editorial Responsibility

The GPT speeds the work. It does not replace the editor's judgment.

THE EDITOR'S CALL Rules 08 – 09
08

Terminology is not optional

The model is trained on these — your job is to confirm they were actually applied:

"Israeli army" ≠ IDF occupied East Jerusalem "claimed" Türkiye ≠ Turkey ISIS (Daesh)
09

Don't trust a quote or number on sight

Even with every rule in place, the model can get a figure wrong or attribute a statement to the wrong source. Verifying attribution and numbers is the core of your role — the GPT speeds the work, it doesn't replace judgment.

THE BIG MISCONCEPTION Rules 10 – 11
10
Memory

The GPT does not learn
from your corrections.

A fix inside a chat lives in that chat only — close the window and it's gone. No memory between sessions. It does not improve on its own.

11

Improvement happens centrally — document & escalate

1 · Input
The exact material you fed it
2 · Wrong output
What the model produced
3 · Expected
What it should have said

↳ then escalate to the team manager. Without the input, nothing can be diagnosed.

THE FIX CYCLE Rules 12 – 13
12

Tell a structural error from a one-off

A recurring error of the same type warrants a change to the instructions. A rare one-off usually doesn't — a rule for every isolated case bloats the GPT and confuses it. That distinction is management's call, not the editor's.

13

Every change is tested before it's adopted

Sometimes a rule fixes one error and creates another. Changes go through a cycle:

Observe→ Write the rule→ Test on examples→ Adopt
